HIDETAKA MIYASAKI - The God of videogames

""Demon’s Souls wasn’t doing well,” he says. “The project had problems and the team had been unable to create a compelling prototype. But when I heard it was a fantasy-action role-playing game, I was excited. I figured if I could find a way to take control of the game, I could turn it into anything I wanted. Best of all, if my ideas failed, nobody would care – it was already a failure.""

2004
  • Hidetaka Miyazaki joins FromSoftware as a game planner for Armored Core: Last Raven
  • Miyazaki works as the main planner for Armored Core 4. Midway in the project, he gets promoted to the game director.
2005
  • Miyazaki works as the director of Armored Core for Answer
  • Demon's Souls development begins under a different director
  • Miyazaki takes over Demon's Souls as the director during its prototyping stag
    • Changes the Camera from First-Person to Third-Person perspective
    • Pivots the gameplay towards battles and exploration and away from being a clone of Elder Scrolls: Oblivion
2008
  • Demon's Souls official reveal
2009
  • Demon's Souls is released in Japan
    • Earns 39,000 sales in the first week in Japan
    • Fails to meet sales expectation in the early months
  • Dark Souls planning begins
    • Demon's Souls is still not recognized in Japan or globally
    • Miyazaki and team just want to make a game to implement their new ideas
  • Demon's Souls is released in North America
    • The game's popularity explodes, sales increase
    • FromSoftware starts to be recognized
